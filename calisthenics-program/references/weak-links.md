# Weak Link Diagnosis & Fix Guide

## How to Use This File

When a trainee reports being stuck on a skill or exercise for **4+ weeks** with no progress:

1. **Identify the skill** they're stuck on
2. **Run the diagnostic tests** for that skill (listed per skill below)
3. **Determine the weak link** — the specific muscle or mobility pattern that's the bottleneck
4. **Prescribe a focused block** of 3–6 weeks targeting that weak link
5. **Return to the main program** once the weak link reaches the threshold

> **Key insight:** Progress in calisthenics skills is only as fast as the weakest link.
> Grinding the skill harder rarely works — identifying and fixing the limiting factor does.

---

## Gym Access

If the trainee has gym access (`GYM`), free weight exercises often allow targeted
overloading of specific muscles that bodyweight cannot isolate effectively.
Note whether the trainee has `GYM` access in Step 1 — it changes the fix options.

---

## Diagnostic Framework

### Two types of limiters:

**Strength limiter** — muscle group not strong enough for the demand
- Symptom: can start the movement but fail at a specific point
- Fix: strength exercises for that muscle group

**Mobility / tightness limiter** — range of motion or flexibility is blocking the pattern
- Symptom: cannot get into the required position at all
- Fix: targeted stretching, mobility drills, and movement prep

Both can coexist. Diagnose each separately.

## SKILL-BY-SKILL WEAK LINK GUIDE

---

### HUMAN FLAG

**Symptom of being stuck:** Can hold tuck flag but cannot extend legs / can't maintain straight body line / shaking or dropping immediately.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Side plank hold | Lateral core endurance | 60 sec each side |
| Copenhagen plank | Hip adductor + lateral core | 30 sec each side |
| Hanging oblique knee raise | Lateral core under load | 10 controlled reps each side |
| Dead hang hold | Grip + shoulder stability | 60 sec |
| Push-up (bottom arm in flag position) | Pushing strength in that arm angle | 10 clean reps |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Lateral Core (most common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Side plank < 60 sec or body sags in tuck flag
- Bodyweight fix: Side plank progressions, Copenhagen plank, windshield wipers, hanging oblique raises
- Gym fix: **Cable/band lateral Pallof press** (resists lateral pull), **Dumbbell side bend** (loaded lateral flexion), **Suitcase carry** (single KB/DB held at side, walk — brutal oblique demand)

**Weak link: Bottom Arm Push (often underestimated)**
- *Diagnosis:* Bottom arm buckles or bends during hold
- Bodyweight fix: Pike push-up variations, handstand wall holds, pseudo planche push-ups
- Gym fix: **Overhead press** (barbell or dumbbell), **Single-arm landmine press** (mimics the arm angle), **Lateral raise** (targets lateral deltoid — key muscle in the flag push)

**Weak link: Top Arm Pull**
- *Diagnosis:* Top arm cannot maintain the downward pull; body tilts
- Bodyweight fix: Pull-up negatives, chest-to-bar pull-ups, archer pull-ups, L-sit pull-ups
- Gym fix: **Lat pulldown** (overload the pulling pattern), **Single-arm cable pulldown** (isolates each side), **Barbell row / Dumbbell row**

**Weak link: Full-Body Tension / Hollow Body**
- *Diagnosis:* Hips sag or pike during hold — body can't maintain rigid plank
- Bodyweight fix: Hollow body holds (3×30–60 sec), RKC plank, dragon flag negatives
- Gym fix: **Ab wheel rollout** (standing or kneeling), **Deadlift** (teaches full-body bracing under load — directly transfers to flag tension), **Farmers carry** (loaded anti-flexion and anti-rotation)

**Weak link: Grip / Wrist**
- *Diagnosis:* Grip fails before core/shoulders
- Bodyweight fix: Dead hang (bar), towel hangs, farmer's carry with bodyweight
- Gym fix: **Farmer's carry**, **Barbell holds** (hold a loaded bar at your side for time)

### MUSCLE-UP (Bar)

**Symptom:** Can do 10+ pull-ups and 10+ dips but cannot complete the transition, or can do it with a kip but not strict.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Chest-to-bar pull-up | Pull height | 5 clean reps |
| Straight bar dip | Push from support position | 10 clean reps |
| Straight bar negative muscle-up | Transition strength | 3 slow negatives |
| False grip hang | Grip position | 20 sec |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Not pulling high enough**
- *Diagnosis:* Bar reaches chin level but not chest level
- Bodyweight fix: Chest-to-bar pull-up training (pull higher each rep), L-sit pull-ups (adds demand), weighted pull-up (use backpack)
- Gym fix: **Lat pulldown** (overload past bodyweight), **Cable pullover** (builds the lat finishing motion), **Weighted pull-up with belt or vest**

**Weak link: Transition (the "punch")**
- *Diagnosis:* Gets chest to bar but can't rotate over — stuck in the transition
- Bodyweight fix: Straight bar dip negatives (from support, lower slowly through transition), jumping muscle-up (use legs to practice the movement pattern), low bar muscle-up (feet on ground)
- Gym fix: No direct gym equivalent — the transition is pattern-specific. Use **dip machine** to overload the pressing portion, and **barbell row** for the pulling portion

**Weak link: Top Push (from support to lockout)**
- *Diagnosis:* Gets over the bar but cannot lock out — arms fail at dip phase
- Bodyweight fix: Parallel bar dip volume (4×10+), ring dip
- Gym fix: **Close-grip bench press** (overloads triceps in the lockout pattern), **Tricep dips machine**, **Skull crushers**

**Weak link: Wrist / False Grip**
- *Diagnosis:* Cannot maintain false grip under load — wrist position breaks
- Bodyweight fix: False grip dead hang (build to 30 sec), false grip ring rows (shift weight forward over wrist)
- Gym fix: **Wrist roller** (builds wrist flexor strength and endurance), **Barbell wrist curls**

### PLANCHE

**Symptom:** Stuck at tuck planche, cannot extend to advanced tuck; or tuck is shaky and inconsistent.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Planche lean hold | Shoulder protraction + core | 20 sec at 45° lean |
| Pseudo planche push-up | Pushing pattern in shoulder position | 8 reps with forward lean |
| Hollow body hold | Core rigidity | 30 sec straight legs |
| Wrist extension range of motion | Wrist mobility | Passive 90° extension |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Scapular Protraction Strength (most common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Scapula wings out or cannot maintain forward lean without sagging
- Bodyweight fix: Scapular push-ups (at top of push-up, push more — protract fully), planche lean with longer holds, ring push-up (forces active stabilization)
- Gym fix: **Serratus anterior cable punch** (extend arm forward against cable — isolates serratus), **Weighted scapular push-up**, **Cable flies** (protracted finish)

**Weak link: Anterior Deltoid / Pressing Strength**
- *Diagnosis:* Shoulders drop forward or fatigue quickly at lean angle
- Bodyweight fix: Pseudo planche push-ups, pike push-ups with forward lean
- Gym fix: **Front raise** (dumbbell, plate — directly loads anterior deltoid), **Overhead press** (builds pressing foundation), **Cable low-to-high fly** (mimics the planche support angle)

**Weak link: Core (can't maintain hollow body in position)**
- *Diagnosis:* Hips pike or lower back arches when trying to extend from tuck
- Bodyweight fix: Hollow body progressions, L-sit, dragon flag
- Gym fix: **Ab rollout** (standing), **Deadlift** (teaches bracing pattern under heavy load — directly transfers to planche tension), **Cable crunch**

**Weak link: Wrist Mobility / Flexibility**
- *Diagnosis:* Wrist pain or inability to put hands fully flat with fingers forward
- Mobility fix: Wrist extension stretch (hands flat, fingers back), wrist circles (slow, full ROM), quadruped wrist rocking, prayer stretch
- No gym equivalent — this is purely mobility. Do not progress planche until pain-free wrist extension at 90°.

### FRONT LEVER

**Symptom:** Stuck at tuck; body drops when attempting to extend one leg.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Dead hang — scapular pull | Scapular depression under load | 10 controlled reps |
| Tuck front lever hold | Core + lat integration | 10 sec |
| Hanging leg raise (straight) | Hip flexor + core | 10 clean reps |
| L-sit pull-up | Lat strength + position | 3 reps |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Lat Strength (most common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Arms bend or body angle collapses immediately when extending legs
- Bodyweight fix: Tuck front lever rows (pull the bar toward hips while in tuck), L-sit pull-ups, front lever negatives (kick to full, hold as long as possible)
- Gym fix: **Lat pulldown** (heavy overload — target 120%+ of bodyweight), **Straight-arm pulldown** (cable — very specific to front lever lat demand), **Barbell row** (heavy horizontal pull overload)

**Weak link: Core / Hip Flexors (can't keep legs up)**
- *Diagnosis:* Hips drop immediately when legs extend — legs feel heavy
- Bodyweight fix: Hanging leg raises (straight), L-sit progressions, hollow body holds
- Gym fix: **Cable pull-through** (hip flexor + core), **Hanging weighted leg raise** (add ankle weights), **Ab rollout**

**Weak link: Scapular Depression**
- *Diagnosis:* Shoulders rise toward ears (elevation) instead of staying packed
- Bodyweight fix: Scapular pull-ups (hang, then depress without bending arms), band pull-aparts (in hang position)
- Gym fix: **Straight-arm pulldown** (teaches depression pattern), **Dumbbell shrug with depression at bottom** (eccentric depression training)

### HANDSTAND

**Symptom:** Can kick up to wall but cannot balance freestanding, or keeps falling in one direction.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Wall handstand hold | Shoulder endurance + alignment | 60 sec (back to wall) |
| Crow pose balance | Wrist loading + balance at lower level | 15 sec |
| Shoulder flexion range of motion | Overhead mobility | Ear between arms when overhead |
| Wrist extension | Wrist mobility | 90° passive |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Shoulder Mobility (extremely common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Cannot get ears between arms when overhead — arms not fully vertical
- Mobility fix: Overhead stretch with band or stick, lat stretch (doorframe), cat-cow, puppy pose (chest to floor, arms extended)
- Gym fix: **Overhead press** (strict — forces shoulder flexibility under load), **Cuban press** (builds external rotation), **Face pull** (rear delt + external rotation)

**Weak link: Wrist Mobility / Strength**
- *Diagnosis:* Wrists tire within 10–15 sec or painful to hold load
- Mobility fix: Wrist extension stretch, wrist circles, quadruped rocking (shift weight over wrists), fingertip push-ups
- Strength fix: Wrist push-ups (on fists first, then fingertips), push-up holds at top

**Weak link: Balance / Proprioception**
- *Diagnosis:* Shoulder strength is fine, wall HS is solid — just can't balance freestanding
- Fix: This is a motor skill, not a strength issue. More practice time: 10–15 min/day, kick-up attempts, pirouette exits, one-arm wall HS taps
- No gym substitute for balance — volume and frequency of practice is the only fix

**Weak link: Core / Body Tension**
- *Diagnosis:* Body bends at hips or lower back during hold (banana shape)
- Bodyweight fix: Hollow body holds (the exact handstand body shape), RKC plank, wall HS with hollow body cue
- Gym fix: **Deadlift** (teaches full-body bracing), **Ab rollout** (anti-extension core)

### PISTOL SQUAT

**Symptom:** Falls backward, cannot reach depth, or extended leg drops.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Single-leg stand (30 sec) | Balance | Stable with eyes open |
| Ankle dorsiflexion (knee to wall test) | Ankle mobility | Knee touches wall at 10 cm distance |
| Hip flexor length (Thomas test) | Hip flexor mobility | Rear leg drops to horizontal |
| Bodyweight squat depth | Mobility pattern | Full depth, heels on floor |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Ankle Mobility (most common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Heel rises off floor when squatting deep — falls backward
- Mobility fix: Ankle dorsiflexion stretch (foot on wall, knee forward), calf stretch (step, heel off), 3D ankle circles, elevated-heel pistol (heel on plate or book) as assisted version
- Gym fix: **Seated calf raise** (builds ankle strength through full ROM), **Tibialis raise** (strengthens the dorsiflexor)

**Weak link: Hip Flexor Mobility / Quad Strength**
- *Diagnosis:* Extended leg drops or pulls into hip flexion bend — cannot keep leg horizontal
- Mobility fix: Kneeling hip flexor stretch, standing quad stretch, couch stretch
- Gym fix: **Leg extension machine** (isolates quad in extended position), **Hip flexor cable raise** (strengthens hip flexors through ROM)

**Weak link: Single-Leg Balance**
- *Diagnosis:* Falls sideways or forward at bottom — not a strength or mobility issue
- Fix: Single-leg stands (eyes closed), single-leg RDL (balance training), box pistol squat (descend to box — removes balance demand temporarily)
- Gym fix: **Single-leg press** (builds confidence in single-leg loading), **Bulgarian split squat** (balance + single-leg strength)

**Weak link: Quad / Glute Strength**
- *Diagnosis:* Collapses at the bottom — not enough strength to stand up
- Bodyweight fix: Box pistol (to lower and lower box), assisted pistol (hold ring or band), Bulgarian split squat volume
- Gym fix: **Bulgarian split squat with dumbbells**, **Single-leg press**, **Step-up with dumbbells** (3×10 each leg)

### PULL-UP (stuck — cannot progress past a certain rep count)

**Symptom:** Stuck at 3–5 reps, or stuck at 8–10 and cannot increase.

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Dead hang | Grip endurance | 60 sec |
| Scapular pull-ups | Scapular depression | 10 controlled reps |
| Australian row | Horizontal pull strength | 3×12 with feet elevated |
| Negative pull-up | Eccentric strength | 3×8 sec descent |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Lat Strength**
- Bodyweight fix: Negative pull-ups (eccentric overload), assisted pull-up (band or jumping), L-sit pull-ups (higher demand)
- Gym fix: **Lat pulldown** (overload — use 110–120% of bodyweight), **Straight-arm pulldown**, **Barbell row**

**Weak link: Bicep Strength**
- Bodyweight fix: Supinated chin-up emphasis, slow negatives
- Gym fix: **Barbell curl**, **Hammer curl**, **Preacher curl** — direct bicep overload rarely needed but sometimes the limiter

**Weak link: Grip**
- Bodyweight fix: Dead hangs (build to 90 sec), towel pull-ups, fat grip variations
- Gym fix: **Farmer's carry**, **Barbell holds**, **Wrist roller**

**Weak link: Body Weight (trainee is simply too heavy relative to strength)**
- This is the most honest diagnosis — reduce bodyweight OR dramatically increase pulling volume over 8–12 weeks
- Gym fix: **Lat pulldown** as the primary pull training until bodyweight catches up

### PUSH-UP (stuck — cannot increase reps or progress to harder variation)

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Push-up test (max reps, full ROM) | Baseline | For reference only |
| Plank hold | Core stability | 60 sec |
| Shoulder external rotation | Mobility | No impingement pain |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Tricep Strength**
- Bodyweight fix: Diamond push-ups, dips, close-grip push-ups
- Gym fix: **Skull crushers**, **Close-grip bench press**, **Tricep pushdown**

**Weak link: Chest (pec minor / sternal)**
- Bodyweight fix: Wide push-ups, ring push-ups (increased ROM)
- Gym fix: **Dumbbell fly** (stretch under load), **Cable fly**, **Dumbbell bench press**

**Weak link: Core / Body Tension (hips sag)**
- Bodyweight fix: Plank holds, RKC plank, push-up with hollow body cue
- Gym fix: **Ab rollout**, **Deadlift** (teaches bracing)

**Weak link: Shoulder Stability**
- Bodyweight fix: Scapular push-ups (protraction/retraction at top), ring push-up (active stabilization)
- Gym fix: **Face pull** (rear delt + rotator cuff), **Band pull-apart**, **Cuban press**

### NORDIC CURL (cannot complete one full rep)

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Single-leg RDL balance | Hamstring + balance | 10 reps each side |
| Negative Nordic (timed) | Eccentric hamstring | Controlled descent > 5 sec |
| Standing hamstring flexibility | Hamstring length | Touch toes with straight legs |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Eccentric Hamstring Strength (most common)**
- Bodyweight fix: Increase negative time (shoot for 8–10 sec), glute bridge holds, single-leg RDL
- Gym fix: **Romanian deadlift** (heavy hip hinge — best direct transfer), **Leg curl machine** (eccentric emphasis — 2 sec up, 4 sec down), **Stiff-leg deadlift**

**Weak link: Hamstring Flexibility (tight, not weak)**
- *Diagnosis:* Cannot lower more than 30° before hamstring cramps or locks
- Mobility fix: Standing hamstring stretch, seated forward fold, supine hamstring stretch with band, leg swings
- This is a mobility block, not strength — do not force the eccentric. Stretch first.

**Weak link: Hip Flexors (pulling hips into flexion)**
- *Diagnosis:* Hips drop/flex during descent — breaks the straight line
- Mobility fix: Hip flexor stretch, kneeling lunge stretch
- Gym fix: **Hip thrust** (builds hip extension strength to resist flexion), **Glute bridge** volume

### BRIDGE (cannot get full back bridge / back bridge push-up)

#### Diagnostic Tests

| Test | What it checks | Pass threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Shoulder flexion overhead | Shoulder mobility | Ears between arms |
| Thoracic extension (foam roller) | Thoracic mobility | Can drape over roller |
| Hip flexor test | Hip flexor length | Rear leg drops in Thomas test |
| Glute bridge hold | Hip extension strength | 30 sec single-leg |

#### Weak Link → Fix Matrix

**Weak link: Thoracic Extension (most common)**
- *Diagnosis:* Can get hands to floor but back stays rounded — load goes to lumbar
- Mobility fix: Foam roller thoracic extension (roll up and down), cat-cow (thoracic emphasis), thread-the-needle rotation, book openings (lying rotation)
- Gym fix: **Thoracic extension over bench** (lie face up, upper back on bench edge, let head drop)

**Weak link: Shoulder Flexibility**
- *Diagnosis:* Hands cannot reach the floor, or arms not vertical in bridge
- Mobility fix: Doorframe stretch (arms wide), puppy pose, overhead band stretch
- Gym fix: **Overhead press** (forces shoulder flexibility under load — stretch component)

**Weak link: Hip Flexor Tightness**
- *Diagnosis:* Hips don't fully extend — bridge looks flat / hips sag
- Mobility fix: Kneeling hip flexor stretch (couch stretch), standing hip flexor stretch
- Gym fix: **Hip thrust** (loaded hip extension — stretches hip flexors at bottom), **Romanian deadlift** (hip extension pattern)

**Weak link: Wrist**
- Mobility fix: Same as planche wrist prep — extension stretches, wrist circles
- Do not push bridge if wrists are painful.

## GYM SUPPLEMENT EXERCISES — QUICK REFERENCE

When a trainee has gym access and is stuck, these are the most transferable free weight exercises
per calisthenics goal. Prescribe as a **3–6 week focused block** (2×/week) alongside reduced
main skill practice.

| Goal Skill | Best Gym Supplements | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Human Flag | Suitcase carry, cable Pallof press, dumbbell side bend, OHP | Lateral core overload, push strength |
| Planche | Front raise, serratus cable punch, ab rollout, deadlift | Anterior deltoid, protraction, core |
| Front Lever | Lat pulldown (heavy), straight-arm pulldown, barbell row | Lat overload past bodyweight |
| Muscle-Up | Lat pulldown, close-grip bench, weighted pull-up | Pull height, transition push |
| Handstand | OHP, face pull, Cuban press | Shoulder mobility + stability |
| Pistol Squat | Bulgarian split squat, single-leg press, tibialis raise | Unilateral quad, ankle |
| Pull-up plateau | Lat pulldown at 110–120% BW, barbell row | Overload principle |
| Nordic Curl | Romanian deadlift, leg curl (eccentric), hip thrust | Hamstring eccentric strength |
| Bridge | Hip thrust, thoracic extension on bench, OHP | Hip extension, thoracic mobility |
| Push-up plateau | DB bench, skull crushers, face pull | Tricep/chest overload + shoulder health |

**OHP** = Overhead Press (barbell or dumbbell)

## FOCUSED WEAK LINK SESSION TEMPLATE

When a trainee needs a dedicated "weak link" session (not their regular training day):

```
## Weak Link Session — [Target Muscle/Pattern] — [Date]

Duration: 30–45 min
Frequency: 2×/week for 3–6 weeks, then retest main skill

### Warmup (5 min)
- [Joint mobility for target area]
- [Light activation exercise]

### Diagnostic Retest (5 min)
- Run the diagnostic test for the identified weak link
- Record result — track weekly

### Main Block (20–25 min)
| Exercise | Sets | Reps / Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Primary gym fix exercise] | 4 | 8–10 | Heavy, controlled |
| [Secondary bodyweight fix] | 3 | 10–12 | Match pattern |
| [Mobility drill if tightness] | 3 | 30–45 sec | Hold, breathe |

### Skill Practice (10 min)
- Maintain 2–3 sets of the current skill level (don't grind — just maintain)
- End on a good rep, not a failure

### Notes
- [What felt weak? What improved?]
- [Retest date]
```
