# Equipment Reference — Difficulty Modifiers & Exercise Availability

## Equipment Catalog

Each trainee should declare which of these they have access to. Multiple items allowed.

| Code | Equipment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `FLOOR` | Floor + wall only | Always available — baseline for all |
| `BENCH` | Bench / chair / box / table | Household item, ~knee height |
| `BAR` | Pull-up bar (straight, fixed) | Doorframe, mounted, or outdoor bar |
| `LOWBAR` | Low bar / Swedish bar / table edge | For rows at torso height |
| `DIPBAR` | Dip bars / parallel bars | Fixed, typically shoulder-width |
| `PB` | Parallettes | Low (floor-level) parallel bars |
| `RINGS` | Gymnastics rings | Height-adjustable, free-swinging |
| `MONKEY` | Monkey bars | Multiple bars for traveling + kips |
| `BAND` | Resistance bands | Modifier — assists or resists |
| `POLE` | Vertical pole / stall bars / two-bar setup | Required for human flag |

---

## Difficulty Modifier System

When the same movement is performed on different equipment, its difficulty changes.
Use this to correctly place a trainee in the right progression step.

### Key rule: Instability = higher difficulty

Rings > Parallettes (low) > Bar/Dip bars > Floor/Bench

But this interacts with ROM (range of motion) and grip angle too — full picture below.

---

## Movement Pattern × Equipment Matrix

For each pattern, equipment options are listed **easiest → hardest**.
Use the trainee's available equipment to find the right column.

---

### HORIZONTAL PUSH

| Difficulty | FLOOR | BENCH | PB | RINGS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | Wall push-up | Incline push-up (hands on bench) | Incline PB push-up | — |
| Beginner+ | Knee push-up | Decline push-up (feet on bench) | — | — |
| Intermediate | Push-up | Push-up | PB push-up (neutral wrist) | Ring push-up (rings low, feet on floor) |
| Intermediate+ | Diamond / Wide push-up | — | PB diamond push-up | Ring push-up (feet elevated) |
| Advanced | Archer push-up | — | PB archer push-up | Ring archer push-up |
| Advanced+ | Pseudo planche push-up | — | PB pseudo planche push-up | Ring pseudo planche push-up |
| Elite | One-arm push-up | — | One-arm PB push-up | One-arm ring push-up |

**Difficulty notes:**
- `PB` vs `FLOOR`: PB push-up is slightly *easier* on wrists (neutral grip) but enables greater ROM (go below floor level = harder than floor at same step)
- `RINGS` vs `FLOOR`: +1–2 difficulty levels due to instability. Ring push-up ≈ Archer push-up on floor.
- Typewriter / lateral: `FLOOR` → `PB` → `RINGS` (all harder due to instability on rings)

### HORIZONTAL PULL (Row)

| Difficulty | LOWBAR / TABLE | BAR | RINGS | MONKEY |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | Australian row (high bar, easy angle) | — | Ring row (high, nearly vertical) | — |
| Beginner+ | Australian row (lower bar) | — | Ring row (lower, more horizontal) | — |
| Intermediate | Feet-elevated row | — | Ring row (horizontal, feet elevated) | — |
| Intermediate+ | Supinated row | — | Ring supinated row | — |
| Advanced | Archer row | Archer row on bar | Archer ring row | Archer on monkey bar |
| Elite | One-arm row (assisted) | One-arm bar row | One-arm ring row | — |

**Difficulty notes:**
- `RINGS` rows are slightly *harder* than bar rows at same angle: rings can swing (requires anti-rotation) and allow full shoulder ROM.
- `RINGS` also enable full retraction of scapula at end of pull — greater training stimulus.
- `MONKEY` bars: similar to fixed bar but grip changes with each rung — useful for unilateral variation.

### VERTICAL PULL (Pull-up)

| Difficulty | BAR | RINGS | MONKEY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | Dead hang | Ring dead hang | Dead hang on monkey bar |
| Beginner+ | Scapular pull-up | Ring scapular pull | — |
| Beginner+ | Negative pull-up (5–10 sec) | Ring negative | — |
| Intermediate | Pull-up (chin over bar) | Ring pull-up | Pull-up on monkey bar |
| Intermediate+ | Pull-up (full ROM, dead hang) | — | — |
| Intermediate+ | Chest-to-bar pull-up | — | — |
| Advanced | L-sit pull-up | Ring L-sit pull-up | — |
| Advanced | Archer pull-up | Ring archer pull-up | Archer on monkey bars |
| Elite | One-arm pull-up (assisted) | Ring OAP (assisted) | — |
| Elite | One-arm pull-up | Ring one-arm pull-up | — |

**Difficulty notes:**
- `RINGS` pull-up ≈ 0.5–1 step harder than bar pull-up (instability, requires shoulder stabilization).
- `RINGS` allow a natural rotation of the wrists during the pull (pronated at bottom → neutral/supinated at top) which is more joint-friendly for elbow health.
- `MONKEY` bars: kipping and swinging moves possible. Brachiation (traveling hand-over-hand) is a unique frontal-plane locomotion pattern.

### DIPS

| Difficulty | BENCH | DIPBAR | PB | RINGS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | Bench dip (feet on floor) | — | — | — |
| Beginner+ | Bench dip (feet elevated) | — | — | — |
| Intermediate | — | Parallel bar dip (assisted/band) | PB dip | Ring support hold |
| Intermediate | — | Parallel bar dip (full) | PB dip full | — |
| Intermediate+ | — | Korean dip | — | Ring dip (rings parallel) |
| Advanced | — | — | — | Ring dip (rings turned out) |
| Advanced+ | — | — | — | Russian dip |

**Difficulty notes:**
- `RINGS` dip ≈ 1.5–2 steps harder than `DIPBAR` dip. Rings require constant shoulder stabilization and the rings tend to flare out — demands much more chest and tricep control.
- `RINGS` turned out at bottom is the standard goal — teaches the correct support position for muscle-ups.

### CORE — ANTERIOR (L-sit / Hollow Body)

| Difficulty | FLOOR | DIPBAR / PB | RINGS | BAR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beginner | Tucked L-sit (knees up) | — | — | — |
| Beginner+ | L-sit (one leg extended) | — | — | — |
| Intermediate | L-sit (floor, both legs) — hardest on floor! | L-sit on dip bars (more clearance = easier) | — | L-sit on bar (hanging) |
| Intermediate+ | V-sit entry | L-sit on PB | Ring L-sit (hardest) | — |
| Advanced | V-sit | V-sit on PB | Ring V-sit | — |

**Critical note — L-sit difficulty is INVERTED:**
- `FLOOR`: hardest (no clearance — requires extreme hip flexor + compression strength)
- `DIPBAR / PB`: easier (legs clear the floor, more height = less compression needed)
- `RINGS`: hardest overall (instability adds difficulty back)
- Program accordingly: a trainee with dip bars who can do L-sit on bars may not yet do it on the floor.

### PLANCHE (Skill)

| Difficulty | FLOOR | PB | RINGS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry | Planche lean | PB planche lean | — |
| Beginner | Tuck planche | Tuck planche on PB | — |
| Intermediate | Advanced tuck planche | Advanced tuck on PB | — |
| Advanced | Straddle planche | Straddle on PB | Tuck planche on rings |
| Elite | Full planche | Full planche on PB | Straddle/full planche on rings |

**Difficulty notes:**
- `PB` planche: slightly *easier* than floor — neutral wrist allows better protraction. PB is the recommended surface for learning planche.
- `RINGS` planche: significantly harder. Even a tuck planche on rings equals straddle on floor in difficulty.

### MUSCLE-UP

| Difficulty | BAR | RINGS | MONKEY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prerequisite | 10+ pull-ups, 10+ dips | 15 pull-ups + 15 ring dips OR bar MU | — |
| Entry | Jumping muscle-up | Assisted ring MU (feet on box) | — |
| Intermediate | Band-assisted MU | False grip pull-up | — |
| Advanced | Full bar muscle-up | Full ring muscle-up | Monkey bar muscle-up (kip) |

**Difficulty notes:**
- Ring muscle-up is generally considered harder than bar muscle-up — the transition is more technical, requiring a false grip and ring turn-out.
- Monkey bar muscle-up uses a kipping technique and is in some ways easier than strict bar MU.

## Equipment Substitution Table

If a trainee lacks specific equipment but wants a similar stimulus:

| Target Exercise | Requires | Substitute | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parallel bar dip | DIPBAR | Bench dip | Easier; less ROM |
| Ring dip | RINGS | PB dip | Easier; no instability |
| Ring push-up | RINGS | Archer push-up | Similar difficulty |
| Ring row | RINGS | Feet-elevated table row | Similar angle |
| Ring L-sit | RINGS | L-sit on PB or floor | Adjust difficulty accordingly |
| PB push-up (deficit) | PB | Push-up on books/dumbbells | Not ideal but works |
| Planche on PB | PB | Planche on floor | Slightly harder on floor |
| Front lever | BAR or RINGS | — | No good floor substitute |
| Nordic curl | Anchored feet | Use table leg or partner | Same exercise |

### HUMAN FLAG

| Difficulty | POLE / STALL BARS |
|---|---|
| Prerequisite | Side plank 60 sec, hanging oblique raises |
| Beginner | Tuck human flag hold 3–5 sec |
| Intermediate | Straddle human flag hold 3–5 sec |
| Advanced | Full human flag hold 2–3 sec |

No equipment substitute exists — the human flag strictly requires a vertical pole or
stall bars. If unavailable, build the prerequisite strength with side plank progressions
and Copenhagen planks.
