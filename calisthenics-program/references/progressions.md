## SKILL PROGRESSIONS

### Handstand
**Phase 1 – Baseline (weeks 1–4)**
- Wall Plank (stomach to wall, shoulders stacked) — hold 3×20–30 sec
- Crow Pose / Frog Stand — 3×10–20 sec
- Wrist conditioning circles, push-ups, extensions

**Phase 2 – Wall Handstand (weeks 4–12)**
- Stomach-to-wall handstand — 3×20–40 sec
- Shoulder taps (wall HS) — 3×5–10 each
- Kick-up practice to back-to-wall HS

**Phase 3 – Balance Work (weeks 8–20)**
- Back-to-wall HS (only heels touching) — 3×20–40 sec
- Kick-up to freestanding (aim for 1–3 sec)
- Wall runs: walk feet up, walk hands out

**Phase 4 – Freestanding**
- Kick-up + hold attempts (quality over quantity)
- Pirouette exits for safety
- Line drills for alignment

**Key cues:** Open shoulders, hollow body, squeeze glutes, gaze between hands (not at floor).

---

### Muscle-Up (Bar)
**Prerequisites:** 10+ clean pull-ups, 10+ straight-bar dips

**Phase 1 – False Grip & Transition**
- High pull-ups (chest to bar) — 4×5
- Jumping muscle-up with bands — 4×5
- Slow negative muscle-ups — 4×3

**Phase 2 – Low Bar / Rings Transition**
- Low bar muscle-up (feet touching ground)
- Band-assisted muscle-up on bar
- Full muscle-up attempts (explosive pull + punch through)

**Key cues:** Explosive pull, lean slightly forward, think "punch the bar down," keep bar close throughout.

---

### Ring Muscle-Up
**Prerequisites:** Bar muscle-up OR 15 pull-ups + 15 ring dips

**Progression:**
1. Ring support hold (rings turned out) — 3×20 sec
2. Ring dips (full ROM) — 4×8
3. Russian dips (ring transition drill) — 4×5
4. False grip dead hang — 3×20 sec
5. False grip pull-up — 4×5
6. Assisted ring muscle-up (feet on box)
7. Full ring muscle-up

---

### Planche
**Prerequisites:** Solid push-up base, wrist prep, pseudo planche push-ups

**Phase 1 – Tuck Planche**
- Planche lean — 3×15–20 sec
- Tuck planche hold — 3×5–10 sec
- Tuck planche push-up (if possible)

**Phase 2 – Advanced Tuck**
- Advanced tuck hold (hips higher, back flatter) — 3×5–8 sec
- Tuck → advanced tuck transitions

**Phase 3 – Straddle**
- Straddle hold — 3×3–5 sec
- Straddle push-ups

**Phase 4 – Full Planche**
- Full planche hold — 3×3 sec+
- Planche push-ups

**Key cues:** Protract scapula hard, depress shoulders, push floor away, posterior pelvic tilt, point toes.

---

### Front Lever
**Prerequisites:** 5–8 pull-ups, good body tension

**Phase 1 – Tuck Front Lever**
- Tuck front lever hold — 3×5–10 sec
- Tuck front lever rows — 3×5

**Phase 2 – Advanced Tuck**
- One leg extended hold — 3×5–8 sec
- Advanced tuck (flat back) — 3×5–8 sec

**Phase 3 – Straddle**
- Straddle front lever — 3×3–6 sec
- Straddle front lever pull-ups — 3×3

**Phase 4 – Full**
- Full front lever hold — 3×3 sec+
- Front lever pull-ups

**Key cues:** Depress scapula, actively pull bar toward hips, hollow body, squeeze everything.

---

### Back Lever
**Prerequisites:** Basic ring/bar comfort, shoulder flexibility

**Phase 1 – Skin the Cat**
- Skin the cat (rings or bar) — 3×3–5
- Hang in German hang — 3×10–15 sec

**Phase 2 – Tuck Back Lever**
- Tuck back lever hold — 3×5–10 sec

**Phase 3 – Straddle Back Lever**
- Straddle hold — 3×5–8 sec

**Phase 4 – Full Back Lever**
- Full hold — 3×3–5 sec

**Phase 2 – Full L-sit**

## LOCOMOTION / GAIT PATTERN PROGRESSIONS

Train the body as a coordinated whole moving through space. Use in warmup or as
conditioning finisher (20–40 m per set).

### Crawling
1. Bear Crawl (quadruped, knees off ground, move forward)
2. Lateral Bear Crawl (side-to-side — frontal plane locomotion)
3. Reverse Bear Crawl (backwards)
4. Crab Walk (quadruped facing up, hips up)
5. Lizard Crawl (low, elbows near ground, opposite arm+leg)
6. Spiderman Crawl (push-up position, bring knee to elbow each step)

### Traveling
1. Inchworm (forward walk-out to plank, walk back up)
2. Inchworm + Push-up
3. Broad Jump (sagittal explosive)
4. Lateral Bound (frontal explosive, stick landing)
5. Rotational Jump (180° turn jump — transverse explosive)

**Programming note:** 2–3 locomotion exercises per week is sufficient. They improve
coordination, proprioception, and movement quality without adding significant fatigue.

## HUMAN FLAG PROGRESSION

**Required equipment:** Vertical pole, stall bars, or two horizontal bars at different heights.
Add `POLE` to equipment catalog when trainee has this.

**Prerequisites:** Strong obliques, shoulder girdle, and lat strength.
Recommended base: 5+ pull-ups, side plank 60 sec, solid hollow body.

**Phase 1 — Structural Prep (weeks 1–6)**
1. Side plank (floor) — 3×30–60 sec each side
2. Copenhagen plank — 3×20–30 sec
3. Hanging oblique knee raises (from bar, knees to one side) — 3×10 each
4. Tuck human flag hold (both knees tucked, body perpendicular) — 3×3–5 sec
5. One-arm lat pull-down simulation (band from high anchor, pull down to hip) — 3×10

**Phase 2 — Tuck Flag (weeks 4–10)**
1. Tuck human flag — 3×5–8 sec (build to 10–15 sec)
2. Tuck flag push (bottom arm pushes, top arm pulls — rocking motion) — 3×5
3. Negative full flag (kick to full extension, hold for 1–2 sec, tuck back) — 3×3

**Phase 3 — Straddle Flag (weeks 8–18)**
1. Straddle human flag hold — 3×3–5 sec
2. Straddle flag + leg sweep (bring legs together slowly) — 3×3

**Phase 4 — Full Human Flag**
1. Full human flag hold — 3×2–3 sec
2. Full flag + hold attempts — build time in 1-sec increments

**Key cues:** Bottom arm fully extended pushing up, top arm pulling down. Body forms a rigid plank — squeeze glutes, hollow body. Head neutral. Engage lats hard on the bottom arm.

**Side note — Windshield Wiper (bar):** A related transverse-plane bar skill.
`Hanging knee wiper → Hanging straight-leg wiper → Full windshield wiper` — 3×5–10 each side.

## INVERTED EXERCISES

### Inverted Rows (Australian Pull-up)
Already covered in Horizontal Pull progressions — but listed here explicitly for clarity.
These are sometimes called "inverted pull-ups" because the body is inverted relative to a standard row.
- Body angle controls difficulty: more horizontal = harder, more vertical = easier.
- Equipment: `LOWBAR`, `BAR`, `RINGS`, `DIPBAR`

### Inverted Hang (Upside-down)
A different meaning of "inverted pull-up" — hanging from a bar with feet up, head down.
**Prerequisites:** Bar hang comfort, core tension, and no neck/blood pressure issues.

1. Tuck inverted hang (from bar, hook knees over bar, hang) — 3×10–20 sec
2. Inverted hang (straight body, legs locked over bar) — 3×15–30 sec
3. Skin the cat — arms, then inverted hang, then return (full shoulder rotation)
4. Inverted pull-up — from inverted hang, pull bar toward chest (reverse pull-up) — 3×3–8
5. Inverted pull-up + slow negative — 3×3

**Key cues:** Lock knees firmly. Keep tension throughout. Exit carefully — never drop.

## INCHWORM & LA LANE (JACK LALANNE) PUSH-UP

### Jack LaLanne Push-up
A push-up variation that emphasizes serratus anterior, triceps, and anterior deltoid.
Hands close together under chest, feet together, body pushes *forward* and *up* rather than straight up.

1. Standard push-up (baseline) — confirm form first
2. Feet-together push-up (narrow stance, increases balance demand) — 3×8–12
3. Jack LaLanne push-up (hands under chest, push forward-up, come to fingertips at top) — 3×5–10
4. Jack LaLanne push-up slow negative (3–5 sec descent) — 3×5–8
5. Fingertip Jack LaLanne push-up (full ROM on fingertips) — 3×5

**Key cues:** The push direction is *diagonal* — forward and upward, not straight up. At the top, you're balanced on fingertips with arms extended. Serratus fires hard. Squeeze glutes throughout.

### Inchworm + Jack LaLanne Combo
A traveling conditioning drill combining locomotion, posterior chain stretch, and pushing strength.

1. Inchworm only — walk hands out to plank, walk back — 3×5 reps
2. Inchworm + push-up — add a push-up at the end of the walk-out — 3×5
3. Inchworm + Jack LaLanne push-up — walk out, perform LaLane push-up, walk back — 3×4–6
4. Inchworm + LaLane + T-rotation — add a side plank rotation after the push-up — 3×4 each

**Programming note:** This combo hits sagittal push, hamstring mobility, and locomotion in one movement. Excellent as a warmup finisher or conditioning circuit element.

## NORDIC CURL (Full Progression)

**Target:** Eccentric hamstring strength. One of the most effective bodyweight posterior chain exercises.
**Equipment:** Anchored feet required — use a partner, door anchor, sofa, or `BENCH`.

**Phase 1 — Eccentric Introduction (weeks 1–4)**
1. Nordic curl negative only (lower as slow as possible, 3–8 sec, catch yourself with hands) — 3×3–5
2. Nordic curl negative with band assist (band under knees helps the ascent) — 3×5
3. Hip hinge in kneeling position (no eccentric yet — just load pattern) — 3×10

**Phase 2 — Partial ROM (weeks 3–8)**
1. Nordic curl to 45° (lower to halfway, return) — 3×5
2. Nordic curl to 60° — 3×5
3. Nordic curl negative (full ROM, hands catch) + band-assisted concentric — 3×5

**Phase 3 — Full Nordic Curl**
1. Full Nordic curl negative + push-up to return — 3×3–6
2. Full Nordic curl (slow negative, pull yourself back up using hamstrings) — 3×3–5
3. Full Nordic curl + pause at bottom — 3×3
4. Full Nordic curl + controlled tempo (3 sec down, 2 sec up) — 3×3–5

**Key cues:** Hips stay extended throughout (no hip flexion). Body is rigid from knee to head. Lower under control — never collapse. The return (concentric) is extremely hard — it's fine to use hands to assist early on.

## LEG BALANCE PROGRESSIONS

Develop proprioception, ankle strength, hip stability, and single-leg control.
No equipment required. Valuable as warmup or between strength sets.

### Single-Leg Stance (Foundation)
1. Single-leg stand — eyes open, 3×30 sec each
2. Single-leg stand — eyes closed, 3×20–30 sec
3. Single-leg stand + arm reaches (reach forward, side, diagonal) — 3×10 each
4. Single-leg stand + head turns (eyes track moving finger) — 3×10
5. Single-leg stand on unstable surface (folded mat, pillow, BOSU if available) — 3×20 sec

### Dynamic Balance
1. Single-leg deadlift (bodyweight, touch floor with opposite hand) — 3×8 each
2. Single-leg squat to box (pistol squat entry — sit and stand on one leg) — 3×5
3. Single-leg hop and stick (hop forward/lateral, land and hold 2 sec) — 3×5 each direction
4. Skater hop (lateral bound, stick the landing) — 3×8 each
5. Reactive balance (partner pushes lightly, trainee must stabilize) — 3×10 pushes

### Advanced Balance Skills
1. Arabesque hold (standing, lean forward, back leg lifts — like a figure skating pose) — 3×20 sec
2. Single-leg Romanian deadlift to balance (slow, controlled) — 3×8
3. Pistol squat balance (at bottom of pistol, pause and hold before standing) — 3×5
4. Single-leg squat with eyes closed — 3×3–5
5. Balance beam walking (any narrow surface — curb, edge of mat) — 3×10 steps

## BRIDGE PROGRESSIONS (Spinal Extension + Hip Mobility)

Bridge trains posterior chain, spinal extension, shoulder flexibility, and hip flexor mobility.
Complements all the anterior core work (hollow body, plank, L-sit).

**Phase 1 — Supine Bridge**
1. Glute bridge (lying, feet on floor, lift hips) — 3×15–20
2. Glute bridge hold — 3×20–30 sec
3. Single-leg glute bridge — 3×10–12 each
4. Elevated glute bridge (feet on bench) — 3×12
5. Hip thrust (back on bench, heavier hip extension) — 3×12

**Phase 2 — Full Bridge Entry**
1. Shoulder bridge (lying, hands beside ears, push to a supported bridge on shoulders) — 3×5
2. Wall bridge (stand 1 m from wall, arch back to touch wall with hands) — 3×3–5
3. Wall walk (from push-up position, walk hands down wall into bridge) — 3×3–5
4. Bridge from floor (full back bridge, hold 10–20 sec) — 3×3–5
5. Bridge hold (build to 30–60 sec) — 3×1

**Phase 3 — Bridge Push-up (Back Bridge Push-up)**
1. Bridge hold — 3×20 sec (prerequisite)
2. Bridge + head lift (lower head toward floor and raise — tiny ROM) — 3×5
3. Bridge push-up (lower head to floor from bridge, push back up) — 3×3–5
4. Bridge push-up (full ROM, slow) — 3×5–8

**Phase 4 — Advanced Bridge Skills**
1. Crab walk from bridge position — 3×10 steps forward/back
2. Bridge to stand (push up from bridge to standing — gymnastic back walkover prep) — 3×3
3. Wheel-out from bridge (walk hands out further, increasing shoulder extension) — 3×5

**Key cues:** Never dump into lower back — push hips high, squeeze glutes. Shoulders stack over wrists. Breathe steadily. Warm up thoracic spine and hip flexors before bridging.

**Mobility prerequisites:** Hip flexors, thoracic extension, shoulder flexion. Add cat-cow, cobra, and thoracic rotations to warmup if trainee is stiff.

## NECK EXERCISES

Neck training is almost universally neglected in calisthenics. Strengthens cervical spine,
improves posture, and reduces injury risk. Train 2–3×/week with light effort and high control.

**Safety rule:** All neck exercises should be pain-free and use slow, controlled movement.
Never use momentum. Start with isometrics before adding movement.

### Isometrics (Beginner — always start here)
1. Cervical flexion isometric — chin to chest, hand resists on forehead — 3×10 sec each direction
2. Cervical extension isometric — hand on back of head — 3×10 sec
3. Lateral flexion isometric — hand on temple, tilt ear to shoulder — 3×10 sec each side
4. Rotation isometric — hand on jaw, resist turn — 3×10 sec each side

### Range of Motion Exercises
1. Chin tucks (cervical retraction — pull chin straight back, double chin) — 3×10
2. Cervical circles (slow, controlled) — 3×5 each direction
3. Lateral neck stretch — 3×20–30 sec each
4. Prone neck extension (lying face down, lift head slowly) — 3×10
5. Seated neck rotation with hold — 3×10 each side

### Loaded Neck Work (Intermediate+)
1. Wrestler's bridge (on neck and heels, bridge position, gentle rocking) — 3×10–20 sec
   ⚠️ Only after pain-free isometrics. Not for beginners.
2. Wrestler's bridge hold — 3×15–30 sec
3. Wrestler's bridge with head movement (small nods forward/back) — 3×5

**Key cues for wrestler's bridge:** Weight on upper traps and base of skull — NOT directly on cervical vertebrae. Hands on floor ready to take weight if needed. Moderate difficulty only.

**Postural pairing:** Always pair neck work with thoracic extension drills and scapular retraction — they address the same postural pattern (forward head + rounded shoulders).

## CARDIO PROGRESSIONS (Bodyweight)

Calisthenics cardio should be progressive and varied across energy systems.
Organize by intensity zone:

### Zone 2 — Low Intensity (conversational pace, 20–60 min)
Best for aerobic base building. Can talk while doing these.
1. Walking (brisk)
2. March in place (continuous, 15–30 min)
3. Bear crawl intervals (30 sec on / 30 sec rest × 8–10)
4. Jumping rope (low intensity — steady rhythm)
5. Step-ups (continuous, steady pace, bench or stairs)

### Zone 3–4 — Moderate-High Intensity (30-second to 3-minute intervals)
Aerobic-anaerobic crossover. Breathing is hard but sustainable for minutes.
1. Jumping jacks — 3×45–60 sec
2. High knees — 3×30–45 sec
3. Mountain climbers — 3×30–45 sec
4. Jump rope (moderate pace) — 3×60 sec
5. Stair running / step-up intervals — 5×60 sec
6. Crab walk + bear crawl circuit — 20 m each × 4 rounds

### Zone 5 — High Intensity (10–30 second max efforts)
Anaerobic. Use sparingly — 1–2 sessions/week max.
1. Burpees — 3–5×10
2. Jump squats — 5×10
3. Explosive push-up (clap or fast) — 5×5
4. Tuck jumps — 5×8
5. Sprint in place (max speed) — 10×10 sec / 20 sec rest
6. Broad jumps — 5×5 (max effort each)

### HIIT Protocols (mix into any session as finisher)
- **Tabata:** 20 sec on / 10 sec off × 8 rounds (4 min total) — pick 1 exercise
- **EMOM:** Every minute on the minute — e.g., 10 burpees every minute × 10 min
- **Ladder:** 1 rep, 2 reps, ... 10 reps of an exercise without rest
- **Circuit:** 4–6 exercises, 30–45 sec each, 15 sec transition, 3–5 rounds

### Recommended Cardio Exercise Progressions by Fitness Level

**Beginner cardio:**
`Walking → Step-ups → Jumping jacks → Mountain climbers → Burpees (half)`

**Intermediate cardio:**
`Jump rope → HIIT circuits → Burpees → Tuck jumps → Broad jumps`

**Advanced cardio:**
`Explosive burpees → Plyometric circuits → Sprint intervals → Jump rope (double-unders)`

**Programming note:** Cardio can be done as standalone sessions (Zone 2 on rest days) or
as session finishers (Zone 4–5, 5–10 min). Avoid heavy leg cardio the day before a
leg strength session.
